01
The Problem

The 3D community
had no home.

Core friction

Scattered across Reddit, Discord, and generic forums

The 3D printing community is massive — and completely fragmented. Creators share to Instagram, ask questions on Reddit, browse Thingiverse, and discuss on Discord. None of these were built for them.

Makers struggled to get discovered, beginners couldn't find focused tutorials, and experienced creators had no way to build a dedicated following around their work.

The gap: No platform existed that combined sharing, discovery, and community discussion — all tailored specifically to the 3D printing world.
No focused discovery Poor recognition Generic platforms

Think Instagram, but built for makers. A visual, community-first space to share, explore, and discuss everything 3D printing.

— Design vision, 3D Hub

The Solution

A dedicated world for 3D printing creators

3D Hub brings together everything scattered across the internet — into one focused, visually rich mobile platform. Share your prints, discover techniques, follow creators you admire, and actually get seen.

Targeted filters for filament types, print techniques, and material reviews mean users find exactly what they need — not a wall of unrelated content.

Spotlight section: Featured creators get dedicated exposure — rewarding quality work and driving community motivation regardless of existing follower count.
02
Research

5 interviews.
4 key fixes.

➕
Simplify the upload flow
Users got confused by separate "upload" and "share" buttons — they expected one action. Consolidated into a single "+" CTA that handles both.
Upload simplified
🗂️
Explore tab felt overwhelming
Too much in one place. Added segmented sub-tabs — Trending, Discussions, and Spotlight — so users can switch contexts without cognitive overload.
Navigation clarity
🔔
Visual feedback in notifications
Users couldn't quickly recognise which post triggered a notification. Adding mini image previews in the notifications tab fixed this instantly.
Visual feedback wins
🔍
Search must be persistent
Users expected the search bar to follow them across every community view — not disappear when switching tabs. Made it globally persistent.
Global search

03
Design Process

Soft surfaces.
Tactile soul.

The neumorphism style wasn't a trend choice — it was a deliberate reflection of the physical, tactile world of 3D printing. Raised buttons feel like something you can press. Inset fields feel like a surface you're carving into. The UI mirrors the craft.

Step 01
Competitive Analysis
Studied Reddit, Instagram, Thingiverse, and Printables to map what worked and what the community consistently complained about.
Step 02
Lo-fi Wireframes
Explored key flows — model sharing, feed browsing, discussion threads, and notifications — grounded in interview insights.
Step 03
Visual Language
Soft neumorphism + Comfortaa typeface + mint palette. Tactile, fresh, and community-warm — nothing cold or corporate.
Step 04
Test & Iterate
Think-aloud sessions revealed navigation and upload issues. Each problem had a direct fix — no guesswork in the solution.
